The following results come from SXLA's organizational profile and reflect documented programme progress as of March 2026.

3 of 3

UCE candidates achieved Result 1

All three 2025 Uganda Certificate of Education candidates achieved the highest possible grade, independently verified by the Uganda National Examinations Board.

277

learners enrolled

Senior 1 to Senior 4 enrolment reached 277 learners by March 2026, growing from two students across four school years.

51.2%

female enrolment

The inclusive enrolment model reaches communities underserved by formal secondary education in western Uganda.

65%

students from refugee families

SXLA serves both refugee and host community students in Kyangwali and the wider Kikuube District region.

15 to 92

computer expansion target

Current computer capacity is 15 operational units, with a target of 92 units by the end of 2027 through the Phase 1 campaign.

USD 295,000

Phase 1 capital target

Funds will support the AI Innovation Lab, dormitory extension, dining hall, solar upgrade, and water infrastructure.

Strategic Targets

2026

Retain 277 students, complete Spectrum Playbook v1.0, reach 92 computers, secure Phase 1 capital, grow to 20 JEP companies, and roll out AI tools to all enrolled students.

2027

Reach 500 students, commission the AI Innovation Lab, launch the Spectrum Innovation Hub in pilot mode, and hold the first SXLA Innovation Challenge and Demo Day.

2028

Reach 550 students, begin Senior 5 and Senior 6 A-Level provision, open Spoke 2 using the Spectrum Playbook, and launch public Hub training.

2030

Operate 10 schools across Uganda with 5,000 or more combined learners and a fully active Spectrum Innovation Hub.
